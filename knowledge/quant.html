1.1 What is Quantum?

Quantum physics deals with systems at the atomic scale, where probabilities and wavefunctions replace certainty.

1.2 Simulation Goals

The goal is to simulate quantum systems that cannot be solved analytically, using numerical techniques.

2.1 Monte Carlo

Monte Carlo methods involve random sampling to estimate quantum probabilities and path integrals.

2.2 Path Integrals

Feynman's path integrals provide a summation over all possible paths that a particle might take, weighted by their action.

3.1 Plotting

Visualization tools help in interpreting simulation outputs like wavefunction distributions and energy levels.

3.2 Interpretation

Interpreting quantum data often involves statistical analysis, phase space plots, and transformation analysis.
